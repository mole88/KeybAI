Geometry is a branch of mathematics that deals with the study of the properties, relationships, and measurements of points, lines, angles, shapes, and spaces. It has been studied since ancient times, with notable contributions from ancient civilizations such as the Greeks, Egyptians, and Babylonians. Over the centuries, geometry has evolved and expanded, with new concepts and theorems being discovered and applied in a variety of fields, including physics, engineering, architecture, art, and computer science.

One of the fundamental concepts in geometry is the point, which is a basic building block for defining other geometric objects. A point is a location in space that has no size or shape and is typically denoted by a dot. A line is a collection of points that extends infinitely in both directions. Lines can be straight or curved, and they are defined by two points or by an equation. An angle is formed by two rays or lines that share a common endpoint, called a vertex. Angles are measured in degrees or radians and can be acute, obtuse, or right angles.

Shapes are another important aspect of geometry, and they are defined by their attributes such as size, shape, and symmetry. Some common shapes include triangles, circles, squares, rectangles, and polygons. A triangle is a shape with three sides and three angles, and it is classified by its angles and sides. Triangles can be acute, obtuse, or right, and they can be equilateral, isosceles, or scalene. A circle is a shape with a curved boundary that is equidistant from its center, and it is defined by its radius, diameter, and circumference. A square is a shape with four equal sides and four right angles, and it is a special case of a rectangle. A rectangle is a shape with four sides, two of which are longer than the other two, and four right angles. A polygon is a closed shape with three or more straight sides, and it can be classified by its number of sides and angles.

In addition to shapes, geometry also deals with spaces and their properties. Euclidean geometry is the most well-known form of geometry, and it deals with flat spaces, such as the plane or a piece of paper. In Euclidean geometry, the parallel postulate states that if a line is drawn through a point that is not on another line, there is only one line that is parallel to the first line. Non-Euclidean geometry, on the other hand, deals with curved spaces, such as the surface of a sphere. In non-Euclidean geometry, the parallel postulate does not hold true, and there can be more than one line that is parallel to a given line.

Geometry is also applied in many fields, including physics, engineering, architecture, art, and computer science. In physics, geometry is used to study the properties of objects in motion, such as the path of a projectile or the trajectory of a planet. In engineering, geometry is used to design and analyze structures, such as bridges, buildings, and machines. In architecture, geometry is used to create aesthetically pleasing and structurally sound designs, such as the Parthenon in Greece or the Burj Khalifa in Dubai. In art, geometry is used to create visually appealing patterns, shapes, and designs, such as the tessellations of M.C. Escher. In computer science, geometry is used to create 3D models, animation, and simulations, such as in video games or virtual reality environments.
